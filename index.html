Build with AI

Build with AI is a hands-on technical event where participants conceptualize, design, and demonstrate an AI-powered prototype that directly solves a real-world problem. The event evaluates the participant‚Äôs understanding of artificial intelligence, machine learning, data science, and practical problem-solving.

Main Purpose

The goal of the event is to encourage students to convert raw ideas into tangible working models. Participants must identify a real issue, propose an AI-focused solution, develop the system, and explain the relevance and scalability of their work.

Round Structure

  • Round 1: MCQ Screening
    A 20 to 30 minute objective quiz containing 25 to 30 questions on artificial intelligence, machine learning algorithms, Python libraries, data science basics, web technologies, logical reasoning, and applications of AI.
  • Round 2: Prototype Development
    Shortlisted teams must build a functional AI-powered prototype. They are required to clearly explain the problem statement, describe the approach, show a working demonstration, and discuss results and future improvements.

Team Requirements

Each team must consist of 2 to 4 members. Participants may belong to different academic years or departments. One team leader must be assigned.

Judging Parameters

  • Innovation and uniqueness of the idea
  • Real-world relevance and practicality
  • Model performance, accuracy, and technical correctness
  • Clarity of explanation, presentation quality, and answers
  • Additional credit for scalability and future scope

CodeWarz

CodeWarz is a multi-round competitive coding event designed to test participants on fundamental programming concepts, data structures, algorithms, debugging skills, and rapid problem-solving abilities. It emphasizes both accuracy and speed.

Event Flow

  • Preliminary Round: MCQ Quiz
    This round evaluates understanding of programming logic, data structures, time complexity, and analytical thinking.
  • Main Rounds for Top 10 Teams
    The main phase contains four sub-rounds, each targeting a specific technical skill:
    • Coding Round (1 hour 20 minutes): Five problems of increasing difficulty. Scored as 5 marks each for two easy questions, 10 marks each for two medium questions, and 15 marks for one hard question.
    • Fill the Algorithm: Participants correct missing or incorrect parts of an algorithm within a limited time. Each correct fix carries separate marks.
    • Rapid Fire: A high-speed question-answer activity that tests reflex-based technical recall.

Team Format

Teams must consist of two members. The rounds are conducted offline in a controlled environment using competitive coding platforms.

Expected Outcome

Participants will gain exposure to real-time coding pressure, algorithmic thinking, debugging efficiency, and structured analytical reasoning.

PromptCraft

PromptCraft is a dual-task event that evaluates both the creative and analytical aspects of interacting with modern AI systems. Participants must craft effective prompts for given situations and distinguish AI-generated text from human-written content based on linguistic and structural cues.

Task Breakdown

  • Task 1: Prompt Writing
    Participants receive three scenarios involving creative writing, content generation, technical assistance, or analysis. The goal is to write the most complete, clear, context-rich, and instruction-perfect prompts that would produce the ideal output.
  • Task 2: AI or Human Identification
    Participants are given short passages of various types such as code explanations, articles, descriptions, and stories. They must identify whether each excerpt is written by a human or generated by an AI model, and justify the reasoning.

Judging Guidelines

  • Prompt clarity, structure, and completeness
  • Use of constraints, examples, and contextual framing
  • Creativity and precision in task interpretation
  • Accuracy in identifying AI versus human writing
  • Quality of justification for each choice

Participation

Participants may join individually or as a team of two. No coding knowledge is required; the event focuses on reasoning, communication clarity, and language analysis.

DreamFrame

DreamFrame is a design-oriented event that challenges participants to transform a given theme or narrative into a visually appealing and meaningful digital poster using Canva. It focuses on creativity, aesthetic balance, clear message delivery, and visual storytelling.

Event Format

  • Participants receive a scenario or theme connected to artificial intelligence, technology, social impact, or creative interpretation.
  • They must create a fully designed poster within a fixed duration of 60 minutes using Canva. All elements including layout, colors, typography, icons, and illustrations should align with the message.
  • A short one to two minute explanation of the design choices must be presented, covering color usage, layout decisions, clarity of message, and reasoning.

Judging Focus

  • Originality and creativity
  • Relevance to the provided scenario
  • Visual appeal, layout structure, and color choice
  • Clarity and impact of the conveyed message
  • Strength of design justification

Participation

Participants may compete individually or in teams of two. No prior design experience is required, making it accessible to all students interested in creativity and visual communication.
